# GENESIS — First 30 Minutes (Beat Sheet) v2
# Last updated: 2026-03-22

*This is the opening of the game. Every second matters. If this doesn't hook someone, nothing else matters.*

---

## 0:00 — PROLOGUE CUTSCENE

**Phase 1: The Ship (5-8 seconds)**
Interior of Adam's ship. Cockpit screens alive, stars visible. EVE's orb hovering at the console. Calm. Professional. Then —

EVE: "Proximity alert. Unknown energy signature. Recommend course correction."

**Phase 2: The Override (5 seconds)**
Adam's hands on the controls (we see hands, not face — keep him semi-anonymous for player projection). A display shows the signal: pulsing, old, strange.

Adam (the ONLY time we hear his voice): "There's a signal underneath it. Someone's alive down there."
EVE: "The energy field is incompatible with our systems. I strongly advise—"
Adam: "Override navigation lock. Take us in."
EVE: "...Acknowledged."

**Phase 3: The Descent (8-10 seconds)**
Ship exterior shot. Small ship entering planet atmosphere. Systems sparking, trailing smoke. Beautiful planet below — greens, blues, alien geography.

Then — a FLASH. Blinding. The screen whites out.

**Phase 4: The Eye (3 seconds)**
Darkness. Then a single mechanical red eye fills the screen. Not a face — just the eye. Ancient. Vast. It's not attacking. It's LOOKING. At the player. Through the screen.

A low sound — not a roar, a resonance. Something that vibrates in the chest.

The eye closes (or the view pulls away). Ship plummets. Impact.

*The player has seen the Dragon before they even know what it is. 3 seconds of red eye seared into memory. Every time they hear that resonance later, they'll remember.*

## 0:30 — TITLE CARD

Black. Silence. Then ambient sound fades in — wind, distant alien wildlife.

**GENESIS**

3 seconds. Fades.

## 0:45 — WAKE UP

Fade in. Adam face-down in dirt. Player gets control. Only action: move. No HUD. No UI.
Tight camera — 10 tiles visibility. World feels huge, you feel small.
Adam stumbles. Injured animation — limping, slow. Suit sparking.
Around him: wreckage, small fires, scattered containers. Alien plants at edges. Unfamiliar sky.

## 1:15 — EVE REBOOTS

Flicker near Adam's shoulder. Static. EVE's orb materializes — glitchy, dim.
"Systems... partial. I—" *static* "—damage assessment in progress. Adam, don't move too fast. Your suit integrity is at 31%."

**HUD appears.** Suit integrity. Battery (low). Health. Minimal.
EVE's orb is flickering. She's damaged too.

**Scan level indicator** appears in corner: small icon showing EVE's current scan mode.
- Level 1: soft blue pulse (passive, always on)
- Level 2: green focused beam (active, player-triggered)
- Level 3: gold resonance rings (deep, stationary)
Visual distinction so the player always knows what EVE is doing.

## 1:45 — CRASH SITE EXPLORATION

6-8 screens of wreckage. Ship hull fragments create walls and platforms.
Player learns basic movement: walk, jump over debris, duck under collapsed bulkhead.

EVE's first Level 1 passive scan. Small alien plant glows — EVE's orb pulses soft blue.
EVE: "Unknown flora. Bioluminescent."
No prompt. No menu. The world is alive and EVE notices.

Player finds cargo container → **Combat knife.** First weapon.
EVE: "Standard issue. At least something survived." (Hint of bitterness.)

## 3:15 — FIRST CREATURE

Small scavenger (alien rat) picking through debris. Sees Adam. Freezes. Non-hostile.

- Approach slowly → skitters away
- Attack → dies easily. EVE's orb dims slightly (micro-reaction, no words)
- Wait → resumes scavenging, pulls something shiny from debris, carries it toward forest

**First trophic encounter.** Decomposer tier. The planet is already consuming your life.

**NOTE: Whether the player kills or spares this scavenger affects minute 27.** If killed, the scavenger nest at the first shelter is empty/hostile. If spared, they tolerate Adam's presence. First invisible consequence.

## 4:15 — SUIT SPARKS

Violent spark. Screen flicker. EVE: "Power cell rupture. The planet's energy field is interfering with organized electrical systems. Anything with circuitry is... degrading."

**Integrity drops 31% → 24% in real-time.** The player SEES it falling.

### What the Tech Suppression Field Actually Is
- Disrupts **organized energy systems** — circuitry, batteries, controlled reactions
- More complex/artificial = faster degradation
- Byproduct of the Dragon's existence (like body heat), not consciously projected
- Closer to Dragon = stronger suppression
- Simple chemistry (fire, gunpowder) works. Muscle/biology works. Electronics don't
- This is WHY the planet rejects technology — it's not malicious, it's NATURE

## 4:45 — ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARD

Toxic spore cloud (alien fungus on hull). Walking through drains health.
EVE: "Atmospheric contaminant. Avoid prolonged exposure."

Three paths:
- Walk through (damage — impatient option)
- Climb over debris (teaches verticality)
- Slash fungus (recoils, clears temporarily, spore burst)

No "correct" option. Player learns: world presents problems with multiple solutions.

## 5:30 — THE SHIP'S CORE

Main hull. Cracked open. Cockpit area. Screens dead. Ship is not flying again.

EVE: "The distress beacon activated on impact. Automated. I couldn't stop it. The Federation will have received it by now."

(Player may not register significance. But it's planted.)

### Sidearm (HIDDEN — requires exploration)
The sidearm is NOT sitting on a pedestal. It's buried in a collapsed storage locker that requires:
- Noticing a partially hidden panel (environmental awareness)
- Breaking or prying it open (knife interaction)
- EVE might do a passive scan ping on the locker if the player is nearby: subtle blue pulse, no dialogue

**Sidearm: 12 rounds.** Chemical propellant (works despite field). Targeting system dead (field killed it).
If found, EVE: "I wouldn't waste them."

### Aiming System
- **Mouse-direction aiming** for all projectile weapons
- Early game (sidearm): precise but ammo-scarce
- Tech tier later: suit targeting assists aim slightly (degrades with suit integrity — aim literally gets worse as tech fails)
- Bio tier: melee-focused, aiming less relevant
- Cipher tier: area effects, homing — aim becomes conceptual

### CO's Message (VERY HIDDEN — requires deep exploration)
A secondary terminal, partially crushed, still flickering. Requires finding a battery cell to power it (maybe from the scavenger area, or hidden in debris). Not a quest — just... there if you look.

The message:

*"I've watched you volunteer for every mission no one else would take. You've always needed to see for yourself what's out there. I can't stop you — I never could. But I want you to know: whatever you find down there, whatever it costs you... the man I'm sending is enough. Remember that."*

Surface reading: military farewell. Deeper reading: a blessing. A pre-mortal charge. "The man I'm sending is enough" — a statement of purpose. The CO knows something, or feels something, about what Adam is walking into. Pre-Fall voice. The last word from the world Adam left behind.

*Both items reward exploration and curiosity. Players who rush get a knife and their fists. Players who look get options and context.*

## 7:30 — FIRST HOSTILE

Exiting ship area — a sound. Bigger than the scavenger. **Predator** (secondary consumer, drawn by fires and scavenger activity).
Alien dog-sized. Fast. Aggressive. Charges.

First real combat. Knife only (if sidearm not found or conserved).
3-4 knife hits to kill. It hurts Adam. Suit absorbs some (integrity drops).

After kill: EVE Level 1 auto-scan — orb pulses blue. "Predatory species. Drawn to thermal signatures."
Pause. "There will be more."

If player uses sidearm: 2 shots. EVE: "...That works too." (Counts remaining rounds on HUD.)

## 9:00 — EDGE OF THE WRECKAGE

Boundary of crash site. Ahead: **The Living Forest.**
Massive alien trees, bioluminescent undergrowth, creatures in canopy. Light rain.
First time the planet looks like something other than a disaster site.

EVE: "Scanning perimeter. The forest canopy is... extensive. I can't determine the boundaries."
Beat. "Adam, I want it on record that I recommended we not come here."

Not angry. Scared. Behind: burning ship. Ahead: the unknown.

## 10:00 — ENTERING THE FOREST

Camera pulls back. Framing opens up. World gets bigger.
Atmosphere transforms. Ship alarms fade → alien wildlife calls, wind, rustling.

EVE scans constantly. Level 1 passives — blue pulses on interesting objects:
"Unusual root structure." "That canopy species is filtering the rain." "Thermal signature, 40 meters, moving away."

## 11:00 — OBSERVATION LESSON

Herbivore (primary consumer) eating from a low plant. Calm.
Plant glows when bitten, releases drifting spores.

If player watches (~5 seconds): EVE does Level 2 scan — orb shifts to green focused beam.
"The spores have a mild regenerative property. The creature knows — it's self-medicating."
Pause. "Adam, if we collected those spores..."

**First bio-tier hint.** No menu. Just EVE noticing the planet has useful things.

## 12:00 — PATH SPLIT

Left: clear path. Scavenger pack (3-4) clustered around something. Not hostile but guarding.
Right: ledge too high for current jump. Something glinting up there.

Left: approach → scavengers scatter → **battery cell** from ship. Restores suit integrity + battery.
Right: can't go. But the player SEES the reward. Lock before key. They'll remember.

## 13:30 — WEATHER SHIFT

Rain intensifies. Not dangerous — atmospheric. Forest responds:
- Plants close up
- Creatures seek shelter
- Bioluminescence increases as sky darkens

EVE: "Barometric pressure dropping. This could intensify."
Her orb shakes off water droplets. She feels physical, present.

Teaching the player: weather is real and changes things.

## 15:00 — FIRST ADAPTED SIGN

Clearing. Center: small cairn of stones. Clearly arranged intentionally.

EVE Level 2 scan (green beam): "These stones were placed deliberately. Recently."
Quiet. "Adam, we're not the first ones here."

Nearby: scratch marks on tree. Too regular. A pattern. Not readable. Just present.

## 16:30 — PREDATOR PACK (Dynamic Event)

Three alien-dog predators. Hunting — stalking something in undergrowth. Haven't noticed Adam.

**Why they don't detect Adam:** The crash site smoke and alien soil have masked his scent. EVE mentions this only if player does a Level 2 scan on the predators: "They're tracking by scent. The crash residue on your suit is confusing their olfactory—" She stops. "Don't move."

### Dynamic Behavior
The pack uses coordinated tactics — one flushes prey from cover, two flank from the sides. (Jurassic Park velociraptor energy.) The player witnesses intelligent hunting.

### Player Choice → Different Outcomes

**Fight (knife — hardest):**
- 3v1 with pack tactics. They flank. Genuinely dangerous
- If Adam wins: EVE deep scan (gold rings) on the alpha → reveals pack hierarchy, territorial marking chemical that could mask Adam's scent in future areas (bio-tier foreshadowing)
- Reward: access to the alpha's kill site which has a **ship component** the pack was using as a den marker

**Fight (sidearm):**
- Effective but uses 3-4 precious rounds. Loud — scatters other wildlife in the area for several minutes
- EVE: concerned about noise drawing larger predators

**Sneak past:**
- Requires patience and reading the patrol pattern
- EVE scan reward: detailed pack behavior data. "They communicate through subsonic calls. If I could replicate that frequency..." (tech-tier foreshadowing — sonic deterrent device later)
- Quieter path leads to a different section of forest with its own discoveries

**Watch and wait:**
- They catch prey. Player watches a hunt play out — brutal but natural
- EVE does the deepest scan here: full ecological analysis. "This is a functioning food chain. Balanced. Ancient."
- After they eat and leave: the kill site has bone fragments EVE can scan for planetary history data
- Emotional beat: the planet isn't hostile. It's ALIVE. You're the intruder

**Each choice gives different scan data and opens slightly different paths forward.** No choice locks you out of progression — just gives different understanding.

## 18:00 — THE GRAPPLE

Deep in forest. Ship debris wedged in a tree: **Grapple module.** Damaged.
EVE patches it. "Partially functional. Battery drain, but better than nothing."

**First major tool.** Battery-powered. Limited uses before recharge.
Feels industrial: mechanical click, motor whirr, cable weight. Adam's world in an alien one.

## 20:00 — GRAPPLE EXPLORATION + VISTA

Short grapple section. Swing across ravine. Pull up to canopy platform.
From up here: the player can see FAR.

- Forest stretches everywhere
- Mountains in distance (Native Ruins)
- Dark stormclouds (Bone Reef direction)
- On horizon: something massive, geometric, WRONG. The Transformed Lands. Not explained

If player looks long enough, EVE quietly: "What IS that?"

## 22:00 — THE SCREAM

A sound cuts through everything. Not animal. Not wind. Enormous. Far away.
Low, resonant call — **the same resonance from the prologue eye.** Screen vibrates.

Every creature freezes. Then scatters — ALL of them, predator and prey, same direction. AWAY.

EVE's readings spike. Orb flares red. "Energy signature — massive — bearing—" Sensors overflow.

Silence. Forest empty. Just Adam, EVE, rain.

EVE: "...I think we should keep moving."

**Player connects the sound to the red eye from the prologue. That thing is HERE. On this planet. With them.**

## 24:00 — FIRST ADAPTED ENCOUNTER (Player-Driven)

**The Adapted doesn't just appear. The player's actions determine IF they see it.**

### Trigger Conditions (invisible to player):
The Adapted has been watching since the Wreckage. Whether it reveals itself depends on:

- **Did Adam kill the scavenger?** Aggressive behavior makes the Adapted more cautious
- **Did Adam fight the predator pack or observe?** Observation = the Adapted sees Adam as a learner, not a soldier
- **Has Adam done Level 2+ scans?** Scanning = curiosity. The Adapted respects curiosity
- **Is Adam wearing full suit or has he removed pieces?** Less armor = less threatening

**If conditions are favorable (2+ of the above):**
The Adapted reveals itself. Humanoid but wrong. Too thin. Skin shifts color with foliage. Watching from behind a tree. Maintains distance.

EVE: "Adam... that's not native fauna. The biosignature is... partially human." Shaken.

It disappears. Leaves behind: a carved chitin piece. Proof it wanted Adam to know.

**If conditions are NOT favorable:**
The player finds ONLY the carved chitin piece — left on a rock in a clearing. No sighting. EVE scans it: "This was crafted. By hands. Recently." The Adapted was there but chose not to be seen. The player knows someone is watching. More unsettling than seeing them.

**Either way, the Adapted presence is established. But the player's behavior determined the depth of the encounter.**

## 26:00 — NATIVE RUINS (Visible, Gated)

Cliff face. At top: carved structures. Ancient. The Native Ruins.
Cliff too sheer. Grapple can't reach.

EVE: "The surface is smooth but there are handholds near the top. If you could get higher, or..."
Trails off. No answer yet.

**Wall-climb gate.** Player sees Area 3, can't reach it. Lock before key.

## 27:00 — FIRST SHELTER + WEATHER CYCLE

Rain heavier. Natural alcove under massive tree root. Dry. Protected.

**If scavenger was spared:** Small nest of scavengers nearby. They tolerate Adam. One brings a shiny bit of debris and drops it near him — a gift? A test? EVE: "I think... it's sharing."
**If scavenger was killed:** Nest is empty. Scratch marks where they fled. The absence is felt.

EVE: "This is defensible. Your suit integrity is at 18%. I'd recommend we stop."

**First save point.** Screen dims. Brief time passage.

### New Day — Weather System Activates
When the screen brightens: **full weather cycle is running.**
- The rain has stopped. Sky color shifted — different time of day
- Different creatures active (nocturnal vs diurnal shift)
- Wind direction visible (particle debris, leaf movement)
- Cloud formations moving across the sky
- Temperature indicator on HUD (affects stamina, creature behavior)
- EVE: "Local atmospheric patterns are... complex. I'll track what I can."

**From this point forward, weather is a permanent gameplay system.** The player wakes into a living world.

## 28:00 — EVE'S OBSERVATION

Preparing to leave shelter. EVE, unprompted:

"Adam... the signal you followed. I've been analyzing it since we landed."
Pause. "It's not a standard distress beacon. The encoding is... old. Centuries old."
"And it's not broadcasting FROM the planet."
Longer pause. "It's broadcasting TO it."

*She says nothing more. No interpretation. No "like an invitation." Just the facts. The player sits with that.*

## 30:00 — THE FOREST OPENS

Forest thins. Canopy opens to sky. Weather system visible — clouds, wind, light shifting.
Ahead: marshland (transitional sub-area). Distance: Bone Reef coast (fog, toxic haze).
Other direction: Ruins cliff, carved architecture above.

Genuine choice. Explore more forest? Approach the cliff? Head to marshland?

The game has started. The player has one question:

**What is this place?**

---

## DESIGN RULES ESTABLISHED IN FIRST 30 MINUTES

1. **Player choice drives story** — not scripted triggers. Behavior determines encounters
2. **Multiple solutions to every obstacle** — no single "correct" path
3. **Exploration rewards depth** — hidden items (sidearm, CO message) for curious players
4. **EVE scan levels are visually distinct** — blue pulse (L1), green beam (L2), gold rings (L3)
5. **Consequences are invisible** — kill/spare the scavenger echoes 24 minutes later
6. **The Dragon is felt before seen** — prologue eye + the Scream. Fear through absence
7. **Weather is real and systemic** — activated at shelter, permanent from that point
8. **Tech degradation is constant** — suit integrity falling creates ambient tension
9. **Scan data varies by approach** — fight vs. observe gives different knowledge
10. **Show, don't tell** — EVE states observations, not conclusions. Player interprets

---

*Every beat serves the three cores: The Fall (crash = Adam's fault), Adam & EVE (relationship under pressure), The Dragon (seen as an eye, heard as a scream, understood as nothing yet).*
