# GENESIS — First 30 Minutes (Beat Sheet)
# Last updated: 2026-03-22

*This is the opening of the game. Every second matters. If this doesn't hook someone, nothing else matters.*

---

## 0:00 — BLACK SCREEN (Audio Only)

Ship alarms. EVE: "Proximity alert. Unknown energy signature. Recommend course correction."
Adam (the ONLY time we hear his voice): "There's a signal underneath it. Someone's alive down there."
EVE: "The energy field is incompatible with our systems. I strongly advise—"
Adam: "Override navigation lock. Take us in."
EVE: "...Acknowledged."

Systems fail one by one. EVE calls out failures. Her voice breaks calm. Last thing before impact: she says his name. Then silence.

*The player hasn't pressed a button. They know Adam (brave, stubborn), EVE (smart, overruled), and that this was a CHOICE.*

## 0:45 — TITLE CARD

Black. Silence. Ambient sound fades in — wind, alien wildlife.

**GENESIS**

3 seconds. Fades.

## 1:00 — WAKE UP

Fade in. Adam face-down in dirt. Player gets control. Only action: move. No HUD. No UI.
Tight camera — 10 tiles visibility. World feels huge, you feel small.
Adam stumbles. Injured animation — limping, slow. Suit sparking.
Around him: wreckage, small fires, scattered containers. Alien plants at edges. Unfamiliar sky.

## 1:30 — EVE REBOOTS

Flicker near Adam's shoulder. Static. EVE's orb materializes — glitchy, dim.
"Systems... partial. I—" *static* "—damage assessment in progress. Adam, don't move too fast. Your suit integrity is at 31%."

**HUD appears.** Suit integrity. Battery (low). Health. Minimal.
EVE's orb is flickering. She's damaged too.

## 2:00 — CRASH SITE EXPLORATION

6-8 screens of wreckage. Ship hull fragments create walls and platforms.
Player learns basic movement: walk, jump over debris, duck under collapsed bulkhead.

EVE's first Level 1 passive scan. Small alien plant glows. EVE: "Unknown flora. Bioluminescent."
No prompt. No menu. The world is alive and EVE notices.

Player finds cargo container → **Combat knife.** First weapon.
EVE: "Standard issue. At least something survived." (Hint of bitterness.)

## 3:30 — FIRST CREATURE

Small scavenger (alien rat) picking through debris. Sees Adam. Freezes. Non-hostile.

- Approach slowly → skitters away
- Attack → dies easily. EVE's orb dims slightly (micro-reaction, no words)
- Wait → resumes scavenging, pulls something shiny from debris, carries it toward forest

**First trophic encounter.** Decomposer tier. The planet is already consuming your life.

## 4:30 — SUIT SPARKS

Violent spark. Screen flicker. EVE: "Power cell rupture. I'm rerouting, but... this planet's energy field is degrading our systems faster than I can compensate."

**Integrity drops 31% → 24% in real-time.** The player SEES it falling. The tech-suppression field is real and eating your equipment.

First emotional beat of the tech tier: your stuff is dying.

## 5:00 — ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARD

Toxic spore cloud (alien fungus on hull). Walking through drains health.
EVE: "Atmospheric contaminant. Avoid prolonged exposure."

Three paths:
- Walk through (damage — impatient option)
- Climb over debris (teaches verticality)
- Slash fungus (recoils, clears temporarily, spore burst)

No "correct" option. Player learns: world presents problems with multiple solutions.

## 6:00 — THE SHIP'S CORE

Main hull. Cracked open. Cockpit. Screens dead. Ship is not flying again.

EVE: "The distress beacon activated on impact. Automated. I couldn't stop it. The Federation will have received it by now."

(Player may not register significance. But it's planted. The clock started.)

In cockpit: **Sidearm** (12 rounds). EVE: "I found 12 rounds. I wouldn't waste them."

Optional: Ship log terminal (barely powered). Mission briefing:
- The distress signal Adam followed
- Planet classified "Quarantine Zero"
- 14 ships entered this system over 200 years. None returned
- Adam volunteered
- CO's message: "You're either the bravest or the stupidest person I've ever served with. Come back alive."

*Optional reading. Player who skips has a game. Player who reads has context. Layer 1.*

## 8:00 — FIRST HOSTILE

Exiting cockpit — a sound. Bigger than the scavenger. **Predator** (secondary consumer, drawn by fires).
Alien dog-sized. Fast. Aggressive. Charges.

First real combat. Knife only (sidearm is scarce — player decides).
3-4 knife hits to kill. It hurts Adam. Suit absorbs some (integrity drops).

After kill: EVE Level 1 auto-scan. "Predatory species. Drawn to thermal signatures — the fires." Pause. "There will be more."

If player uses gun: 2 shots. EVE: "...That works too. Seven rounds remaining." (Mild judgment.)

## 9:30 — EDGE OF THE WRECKAGE

Boundary of crash site. Ahead: **The Living Forest.**
Massive alien trees, bioluminescent undergrowth, creatures in canopy. Light rain.
First time the planet looks like something other than a disaster site.

EVE: "Scanning perimeter. The forest canopy is... extensive. I can't determine the boundaries."
Beat. "Adam, I want it on record that I recommended we not come here."

Not angry. Scared. Telling Adam — and the player — this was his choice.

Behind: burning ship. Ahead: the unknown.

## 10:00 — ENTERING THE FOREST

Camera pulls back. Framing opens up. World gets bigger.
Atmosphere transforms. Ship alarms fade → alien wildlife calls, wind, rustling.

EVE scans constantly. Level 1 passives firing on everything:
"Unusual root structure." "That canopy species is filtering the rain." "Thermal signature, 40 meters, moving away."

The world is rich and she can't stop noticing.

## 11:00 — OBSERVATION LESSON

Herbivore (primary consumer) eating from a low plant. Calm. Not a threat.
Plant glows when bitten, releases drifting spores.

If player watches (~5 seconds): EVE Level 2 scan unprompted.
"The spores have a mild regenerative property. The creature knows — it's self-medicating."
Pause. "Adam, if we collected those spores..."

**First bio-tier hint.** No menu. No crafting screen. Just EVE noticing the planet has things Adam could use.

## 12:00 — PATH SPLIT

Left: clear path. Scavenger pack (3-4) clustered around something. Not hostile but guarding.
Right: ledge too high for current jump. Something glinting up there.

Left: approach → scavengers scatter → **battery cell** from ship. Restores suit integrity + battery.
Right: can't go. But the player SEES the reward. Lock before key. They'll remember this spot.

## 13:30 — WEATHER SHIFT

Rain intensifies. Not dangerous — atmospheric. Forest responds:
- Plants close up
- Creatures seek shelter
- Bioluminescence increases as sky darkens

EVE: "Barometric pressure dropping. This could intensify."
Her orb shakes off water droplets. Tiny animation. She feels physical, present.

Teaching the player: weather is real and changes things.

## 15:00 — FIRST ADAPTED SIGN

Clearing. Center: small cairn of stones. Clearly arranged intentionally.

EVE Level 2 scan: "These stones were placed deliberately. Recently — within the last few planetary cycles."
Quiet. "Adam, we're not the first ones here."

Player knows this from ship log (14 ships, 200 years). But knowing ≠ SEEING evidence.
Someone survived. Someone built this.

Nearby: scratch marks on tree. Too regular. A pattern. Not readable. Just present.

## 16:30 — PREDATOR PACK

Three alien-dog predators. Hunting — stalking something in undergrowth. Haven't noticed Adam.

Options:
- Fight (3 at once with knife — hard but possible, costly)
- Sidearm (effective but loud — attracts more?)
- Sneak past (path through dense foliage)
- Watch (they catch prey, eat, leave. EVE scans: "Coordinated pack behavior. Subsonic communication.")

## 18:00 — THE GRAPPLE

Deep in forest. Ship debris wedged in a tree: **Grapple module.** Damaged.
EVE patches it. "Partially functional. It'll drain the battery, but better than nothing."

**First major tool.** Battery-powered. Limited uses before recharge.
Reach higher platforms, cross gaps, pull small objects.

The grapple FEELS like technology: mechanical click, motor whirr, cable weight. Industrial. Adam's world used in an alien one.

## 20:00 — GRAPPLE EXPLORATION + VISTA

Short grapple section. Swing across ravine. Pull up to canopy platform.
From up here: the player can see FAR.

- Forest stretches everywhere
- Mountains in distance (Native Ruins)
- Dark stormclouds (Bone Reef direction)
- On the horizon: something massive, geometric, wrong. The Transformed Lands. Not explained

If player looks long enough, EVE quietly: "What IS that?"

## 22:00 — THE SCREAM

A sound cuts through everything. Not animal. Not wind. Enormous. Far away.
Low, resonant call. Screen vibrates slightly.

Every creature freezes. Then scatters — ALL of them, predator and prey, same direction. AWAY.

EVE's readings spike. Orb flares. "Energy signature — massive — bearing—" Can't finish. Sensors overflow.

Silence. Forest empty. Just Adam, EVE, rain.

EVE: "...I think we should keep moving."

**First Dragon experience.** Not seen. HEARD. FELT. Everything on the planet fears it.

## 24:00 — FIRST ADAPTED ENCOUNTER

Forest eerily quiet (animals fled). Movement — a figure. Humanoid but wrong. Too thin. Skin shifts color.

Watching Adam from behind a tree. Approaches → retreats. Maintaining distance. Studying him.

EVE: "Adam... that's not native fauna. The biosignature is... partially human." Shaken. "How is that possible?"

Figure disappears. Left behind: a carved chitin piece at the base of the tree.
Not useful. Not a weapon. Proof it wanted Adam to know it was there.

## 26:00 — NATIVE RUINS (Visible, Gated)

Cliff face. At top: carved structures. Ancient. The Native Ruins.
Cliff too sheer. Grapple can't reach.

EVE: "The surface is smooth but there are handholds near the top. If you could get higher, or..."
Trails off. No answer yet.

**Wall-climb gate.** Player sees Area 3, can't reach it. Needs observation/bio unlock later.

## 27:00 — FIRST SHELTER

Rain heavier. Natural alcove under massive tree root. Dry. Protected.
Scavenger nest nearby — they tolerate Adam now.

EVE: "This is defensible. Your suit integrity is at 18%. I'd recommend we stop."

**First save point.** Screen dims. Brief time passage. Rain stops. Forest sounds different — new creature shift.

27 minutes played. Fought, explored, discovered, been scared. Have knife, sidearm (limited), grapple, questions. Seen evidence of visitors, met something humanoid, heard the Dragon, equipment is dying.

## 28:00 — EVE'S REVELATION

Preparing to leave shelter. EVE, unprompted:

"Adam... the signal you followed. I've been analyzing it since we landed."
Pause. "It's not a standard distress beacon. The encoding is... old. Centuries old."
"And it's not broadcasting FROM the planet."
Pause. "It's broadcasting TO the planet. Like... an invitation."

No follow-up. No explanation. Information dropped like a stone into water.

Adam followed a signal that wasn't calling for help. It was calling for HIM.

## 30:00 — THE FOREST OPENS

Forest thins. Canopy opens to sky. Ahead: marshland (transitional sub-area).
Distance: Bone Reef coast (fog, toxic haze). Other direction: Ruins cliff, carved architecture above.

Genuine choice. Explore more forest? Approach the cliff? Head to marshland?

The game has started. The player has one question:

**What is this place?**

---

*Every beat serves the three cores: The Fall (crash = Adam's fault), Adam & EVE (relationship established under pressure), The Dragon (heard but not seen, already feared).*
