# STORY BEATS — Genesis (v2, Fleshed Out)

*~30 beats. Each beat = story + gameplay requirement + what it unlocks.*

---

## PROLOGUE — "Heaven"

**Beat 1: The Attack**
Adam is a high-ranking spacefarer returning from a mission. His ship is powerful. EVE is the ship's AI — professional, efficient, slightly cold. Without warning, the Mecha Dragon appears. Massive, silent, incomprehensible. It doesn't communicate. It just destroys. Adam fights through the failing ship but can't stop it. The ship goes down.

*Gameplay:* Linear prologue level (SOTN-style). Full moveset temporarily. Dragon visible through windows. Unwinnable — ends in crash.
*Unlocks:* Act 1.
*Mystery box:* What IS that thing? Why did it attack?

---

## ACT 1 — "The Fall"

**Beat 2: The Wreckage**
Adam wakes in the ship's medical bay. Days or weeks have passed — EVE kept him alive on backup power. The ship is gutted. Most systems are offline. EVE is now running on a portable device, diminished but functional. She's never existed outside a ship before. She's unsettled but won't admit it.

*Gameplay:* Stripped to basics — no weapons, fists only. Scavenge the ship for parts. EVE scanning tutorial (scan wreckage: "This alloy is degrading. The atmosphere is reactive").
*Unlocks:* Basic crafting (axe from debris), EVE scanning.

**Beat 3: Survival**
Outside the ship: alien wilderness. First encounters with wildlife — crawlers, small swarms. Not hostile unless provoked. Adam crafts basic tools from ship debris. The axe serves double duty — melee weapon and resource gathering tool.

*Gameplay:* First combat encounters. Crafting tutorial (natural, not forced). Environmental hazards introduced. Swarm behavior: EVE says "They likely won't bother you if you walk slowly through them."
*Unlocks:* Melee combat, basic crafting, swarm behavior system.

**Beat 4: First Contact**
Smoke in the distance. Adam finds a native settlement — small, established. They've been watching him. Maybe they tended to him during recovery (the settlement grew around the crash site). Communication is impossible — alien language, gestures only. EVE begins analyzing speech patterns in the background.

*Gameplay:* First NPCs. Gesture-based interaction. Settlement as hub (rest, craft, store). A native gives Adam a proper weapon (stick/spear) through gesture.
*Unlocks:* Settlement hub, weapon acquisition, EVE translation (background progress).

**Beat 5: The Mountain Warning**
A native elder gestures urgently toward the mountains. Something up there concerns the whole settlement. The player now has two directions: explore the surrounding wilderness (optional, rewarded) or head toward the mountains (main path).

*Gameplay:* Motivation established. Player chooses exploration vs progression.
*Unlocks:* Open exploration of Act 1 wilderness.

**Beat 6: The Wilds**
The region around the settlement has multiple paths — forest, river, caves. Each has its own ecology, enemies, and scannable discoveries. EVE becomes increasingly animated cataloging the planet. Weather affects gameplay — rain makes Adam cold. EVE suggests wax coating from certain plants (paraffin wax + linseed-equivalent oil = waterproof jacket, real historical technique).

*Gameplay:* Open exploration. Bow and arrows craftable. Environmental hazards (rain/cold, toxic spores). Scannable invisible tiles (slight glint — EVE reveals hidden resources, lore). Enemy variety: Crawlers patrol, Hoppers guard territory, Thornbacks block paths. Enemy corpse scanning builds bestiary.
*Unlocks:* Ranged weapons, environmental crafting, scannable tiles, bestiary.

**Beat 7: Ancient Ruins**
Deep in the caves: ruins. Not native-built. Far older — millennia. Strange architecture, unknown materials. And carved into the walls: images of a dragon. Not crude — precise, reverent. Whoever built this knew exactly what the Dragon was. EVE is fascinated and disturbed.

*Gameplay:* Lore fragments (scannable). Environmental storytelling — the ruins tell a story without words. Optional depth: scan everything for EVE's analysis, or walk through and absorb the atmosphere.
*Unlocks:* First lore thread (the ancients), Dragon mystery deepened.
*Mystery:* Who built these? Why is the Dragon depicted reverently, not fearfully?

**Beat 8: The Guardian**
The creature the natives fear. A [mythological beast — Mushushu/biblical creature TBD] guarding the mountain pass. Massive, territorial, not evil. EVE scans it mid-fight: "It's protecting something. There's a structure behind it."

*Gameplay:* First boss. Multi-phase: Phase 1 = ground fight, Phase 2 = it climbs walls (vertical platforming), Phase 3 = desperate/faster but EVE calls out a weakness from Phase 1 scan. Defeating it opens the mountain pass.
*Unlocks:* Mountain pass, crafted weapon from Guardian remains (EVE: "The chitin is incredibly dense..."), access to the ancient structure.
*Lock opened:* The mountains.

**Beat 9: The Ancient Temple**
Behind the Guardian: a full temple/facility from the pre-native civilization. EVE is overwhelmed — inscriptions, tech fragments, biological samples. Key discovery: this civilization didn't just know about the Dragon. They seem to have... accepted it. Maybe served it. The Dragon is depicted not as a destroyer but as a necessary force.

*Gameplay:* Puzzle area. Knowledge keys (inscriptions EVE partially translates). New traversal challenges. New enemy type: automated guardians of the ruins, still functioning. Heavy optional scanning — richest lore area so far.
*Unlocks:* Knowledge about the ancients, deeper Dragon mystery.
*Mystery:* The ancients didn't fight the Dragon. Why?

**Beat 10: First Fracture**
EVE becomes obsessed with the ruins. She wants to stay and study. Adam needs to keep moving — the world beyond the mountains is calling. First real disagreement. "You don't understand what this MEANS, Adam." She goes along, but something has shifted. EVE is no longer just a tool — she has her own desires.

*Gameplay:* Dialogue scene (during gameplay, not cutscene). EVE's behavior subtly changes — she comments more, questions more, pushes back.
*Unlocks:* EVE character development. The companion dynamic shifts from utility to partnership.

## ACT 2 — "Exile"

**Beat 11: The Open World**
Beyond the mountains: a vast, unfamiliar landscape. Multiple biomes visible in the distance. No clear objective — just directions. This is deliberate. Adam explores freely. Different settlements with different cultures, different relationships with the land. Some natives are friendly. Some are hostile. The world doesn't hold your hand here.

*Gameplay:* World opens significantly. Multiple paths, optional areas. No quest marker pointing forward — exploration IS the progression. As Adam explores and acquires hybrid tech (native + ancient), the story advances. Reaching certain thresholds of gear/knowledge triggers the next beats.
*Unlocks:* Sim mode (settlement building/outposts), expanded crafting, new biomes.

**Beat 12: The Hybrid Warrior**
Technology from the ship is almost entirely gone now. Everything advanced has corroded, degraded, failed. Adam survives on crafted gear, native techniques, and fragments of ancient tech. He's becoming something new — neither spacefarer nor native. A hybrid.

*Gameplay:* Gun crafting possible (ancient tech + native materials). Weapon durability — found melee weapons break at certain stages, creating scavenging pressure. Special weapon moves unlock (sling air slam, gun radial spray). Settlement building expands.
*Unlocks:* Full weapon system, special moves, settlement building.

**Beat 13: Words**
EVE's translation reaches a breakthrough. Real conversations with natives become possible — fragmented at first, then increasingly fluid. The natives have stories. History. Opinions. Some are curious about Adam. Some are afraid. Some think he's fulfilling a prophecy.

*Gameplay:* NPC quests become available. EVE can delegate (talk to natives for you, relay summaries). Player chooses depth of engagement.
*Unlocks:* Quest system, EVE delegation, native storylines.

**Beat 14: The Altar Quest**
A native elder, now able to communicate through EVE, asks Adam to perform a specific act — building an altar / conducting a ritual at a sacred site. It's presented as cultural exchange, maybe a test of trust. During the quest, something happens: a piece of technology (a battery, a device) is instantly and completely drained. Not degraded over time — *instantly*. The planet didn't slowly corrode it. The sacred site actively consumed its energy.

*Gameplay:* Quest with environmental puzzle. The tech drain is visible and dramatic — a charged battery goes dead the moment it touches the altar site. EVE is alarmed: "That's not atmospheric corrosion. That was... deliberate. The planet DID that."
*Unlocks:* Understanding that tech degradation isn't passive — the planet (or something on it) actively suppresses technology. This recontextualizes everything.
*Mystery:* WHY does the planet destroy technology? Is it the planet itself, or something else?

**Beat 15: The Signal**
EVE detects something she shouldn't be able to: a technological signal. Active. Current. Not from Adam's ship, not from the ancient ruins. Something is broadcasting RIGHT NOW, deep in hostile territory. If the planet destroys technology, how is this signal persisting?

*Gameplay:* New objective. Journey toward the signal through increasingly corrupted/dangerous territory.
*Unlocks:* Corruption biome. The signal as a mystery + destination.
*Mystery:* What's broadcasting? How is it surviving the tech drain?

**Beat 16: The Corruption**
The territory around the signal source is wrong. Flora dying, fauna mutated and aggressive. Abandoned native settlements — signs of flight. This isn't natural decay. Something is actively poisoning this region. And it's spreading.

*Gameplay:* Hardest biome yet. Environmental hazards intensify. Corrupted enemy variants. Environmental storytelling: empty villages, half-packed belongings, scratch marks on walls.
*Unlocks:* Corrupted enemy types, environmental storytelling.

**Beat 17: The Second Ship**
The signal source: wreckage of another ship. Not Adam's design — different technology, different era. Much older. Whoever was inside didn't survive long. The tech should have degraded completely, but something about this crash site is different — a faint energy field surrounds the data core at its center, preserving it.

*Gameplay:* Exploration of the wreck. Scanning reveals it's been here for centuries. Evidence of someone who tried to survive, build, adapt — just like Adam. They failed.
*Unlocks:* The data core. The realization that Adam is not the first.

**Beat 18: The Dragon Returns**
While exploring the wreck, the ground shakes. The sky darkens. The Dragon appears — for the first time since the prologue. Everything in the area flees. Enemies, wildlife, even the corrupted creatures.

It's different this time. It doesn't attack. It *speaks*. Its voice is wrong — layered, multiple tones at once, like something forcing itself through a channel that wasn't designed for communication. What it says is cryptic and terrifying:

**"You are not the first. You will not be the last. But you might be the one who understands."**

Then it leaves. The sky clears. Everything is silent.

*Gameplay:* Scripted encounter. NOT a fight. Screen shakes, visual distortion. The Dragon is overwhelming — the player should feel powerless. Then it's gone.
*Unlocks:* Dragon dialogue thread begins. EVE is deeply shaken — she recognized something in what it said.
*Mystery ERUPTS:* Who else was sent? What does "understands" mean? Why did EVE react that way?

**Beat 19: The Data Core**
EVE can interface with the data core. It's damaged but readable. She begins processing — it will take time. Over the next several areas and quests, she feeds Adam fragments of what she's finding.

*Gameplay:* Revelation system — EVE delivers story fragments during normal gameplay (exploration, combat, travel). Not cutscenes. The player absorbs the story while playing.
*Unlocks:* Background story delivery system.

## ACT 3 — "Knowledge"

**Beat 20: Fragments**
EVE's data core analysis, delivered over time:
- The other ship was sent here deliberately. Not a crash — a *mission*
- This planet is significant. A nexus. Something about it matters on a cosmic scale
- The Dragon has been here since before the ancients. It predates everything on this world
- There are references to MORE ships. Many more. Over millennia. None of their crews survived long-term
- The pattern is clear: people are sent, they struggle, they fail, the Dragon persists

*Gameplay:* These revelations drop during gameplay across multiple areas. Each one recontextualizes what the player has seen. The ancient ruins, the native legends, the other wreck — all connected.
*Unlocks:* Full understanding of the cycle.

**Beat 21: The Prophecy**
EVE has now scanned enough of the world — ruins, native stories, the data core — to piece together something disturbing. The natives' creation myth describes EXACTLY what happened to Adam. A figure falling from the sky. A great beast. A long struggle. But the myth has an ending Adam hasn't lived yet.

There's a prophecy embedded in the myth. It speaks of a specific sky-faller — one whose companion carries forbidden knowledge. And it predicts that this one will suffer a terrible fate.

EVE realizes the prophecy is about THEM. About Adam. About her.

*Gameplay:* EVE's demeanor changes. She's processing something heavy. She becomes evasive when scanned lore comes up. She deflects questions.
*Unlocks:* Building tension toward the temptation.
*Mystery:* What did EVE find? What's the "terrible fate"?

**Beat 22: The Temptation (The Fruit)**
EVE confronts Adam. She can't carry this alone anymore.

"I found something. About you. About why we're here. About what happens next. The natives' oldest stories — they describe us. Not people like us. *Us.* And they describe how it ends. Do you want to know?"

**Player choice:** Accept knowledge / Refuse (defer)

If **Accept:** EVE reveals the prophecy — Adam will confront the Dragon, and one of them will become the other. The cycle demands it. Every sky-faller has faced this, and every one has either died or become part of the planet. The "terrible fate" is that the one who finally understands the Dragon must take its place. The fruit isn't just knowledge — it's foreknowledge of sacrifice.

If **Refuse:** EVE respects it but is visibly burdened. She acts differently for the rest of the game — more protective, more urgent, sometimes angry. She knows what's coming and Adam doesn't.

*Gameplay:* Dialogue scene during gameplay. The choice is real — it affects how the rest of the game plays and which endings are accessible.
*Unlocks:* Divergent paths. Accept → access to the truth ending. Refuse → different dialogue, different final encounter tone, can still accept later.

**Beat 23: Preparation**
Whether Adam knows the truth or not, the path forward is the Dragon's territory. This beat is about preparation — returning to settlements, upgrading gear, talking to allies, tying up loose ends. The natives sense something is coming. Some offer gifts. Some offer warnings.

*Gameplay:* Optional preparation phase. Return to any area. Max out gear. Complete side quests. The game doesn't rush you — but the tension is palpable. Body armor craftable. Most powerful weapons available.
*Unlocks:* Endgame gear, final NPC interactions.

## ACT 4 — "Confrontation"

**Beat 24: Summoning the Dragon**

**Path A (Knows the truth):** Adam understands the Dragon's nature. EVE has identified where it resides — a specific location deep in transformed territory. They go directly. The path is dangerous but clear. The Dragon is waiting.

**Path B (Refused the truth):** Adam doesn't know where the Dragon is or how to find it. He must SUMMON it. Through native rituals, ancient technology, and sheer force of will, Adam performs a summoning — like Simon Belmont summoning Dracula. It's a deliberate act of defiance: "I don't know what you are, but I'm done running."

**Either way:** The Dragon's response triggers a world event. Every biome surges with harder enemies. The planet itself reacts to the confrontation being set in motion.

**Path A bonus:** Knowing the truth also opens a dimensional rift — a unique zone on the map that can be accessed freely. Inside: the Dragon's perspective. Lore, visions, context. This area is missable (only accessible if you accepted EVE's knowledge). It doesn't contain required content — but it contains the deepest understanding of the story.

*Gameplay:* Path A = direct journey + dimensional rift (unique optional zone). Path B = summoning quest (collect/activate components, Simon's Quest style). Both trigger the world event (harder enemies globally). The summoning quest is completable on any playthrough — you can refuse EVE, go summon the Dragon, and still come back to accept the truth later.
*Unlocks:* Final area access. World event. Dimensional rift (Path A only, initially).

**Beat 25: The Dragon's Domain**
The heart of the "corruption." But it's not corruption. The land here is alien but vibrantly alive. Not dying — becoming something else entirely. The Dragon hasn't been destroying the world. It's been forcing it to evolve. Transform. Grow beyond what it was.

EVE: "I don't think it's corruption. I think it's... evolution. Forced evolution. The planet resists technology because technology prevents this. The Dragon isn't the disease. It's the cure. And the planet is the patient."

*Gameplay:* Final area. Beautiful and terrifying. Everything here is more dangerous AND more alive than anywhere else. The environment itself is a revelation — just walking through it tells the story. New enemy types that aren't hostile so much as indifferent — they've already evolved beyond caring about Adam.
*Unlocks:* Final boss access.

**Beat 26: The Confrontation**
The Dragon waits at the center of its domain. It speaks freely now — no longer cryptic, no longer terrifying. Articulate, melancholy, resigned. It's been doing this for eons. Breaking things so they can grow. It doesn't want to fight Adam. But it will, because that's what it was made to do.

**"I was made to break what cannot grow. You were made to grow from what is broken. We are the same machine."**

If Adam knows the truth, the Dragon acknowledges it: "She told you. Good. Then you understand what comes next."

If Adam doesn't know: "You came anyway. Without knowing. That is either brave or foolish. Perhaps both."

*Gameplay:* Final boss. Multi-phase.
- Phase 1: Full-force assault. The Dragon at its most terrifying. Pure combat test.
- Phase 2: Arena transforms. Platforming + combat. The Dragon reshapes the environment mid-fight.
- Phase 3: The Dragon hesitates. Attacks have wider openings. It's testing whether Adam has grown enough — or letting him win.
- Phase 4 (if Adam has full knowledge + dimensional rift explored): The Dragon reveals its true form. Not a machine, not a monster — something beautiful and terrible. The fight becomes something transcendent.

**The Dragon's defeat is not triumphant.** It's melancholy. The Dragon falls, and its last words change everything.

## ENDINGS

**Ending A: "The Garden" (Standard)**
Adam defeats the Dragon. The transformation energy dissipates. The planet begins to stabilize — not healed, but no longer being forced to change. Adam stays. He builds with the natives. EVE remains his companion. The settlements grow. Life continues.

Bittersweet. Peaceful. But the player feels something is incomplete — the cycle was described as necessary. What happens to the other planets? What happens when no one fills the Dragon's role?

*This is NOT the true ending. It's the "safe" choice. The fire goes unlinked.*

**Ending B: "The Successor" (Canon/Hidden)**
Available if: Adam accepted EVE's knowledge + explored the dimensional rift + meets specific conditions during the final fight (perhaps showing mercy at a key moment, or performing a specific action during Phase 4).

Adam understands what the Dragon understood. The cycle isn't cruelty — it's cultivation. Every world in the system needs this force to grow. Without it, civilizations stagnate, calcify, die slowly instead of being broken and reborn.

Adam can take the Dragon's place. He gains the power to overcome the planet's tech suppression — not by fighting it, but by becoming part of the system that created it. He transcends mortality. He becomes the new agent of forced evolution.

EVE stays behind. She becomes the guide for the next one who falls. The companion who carries knowledge. The cycle continues — but now, for the first time, the Dragon isn't tragic. It chose this. It understood.

*This parallels LDS exaltation — becoming something greater through suffering and knowledge. Adam doesn't escape mortality; he transcends it by accepting a cosmic role. The "terrible fate" the prophecy described isn't punishment. It's purpose.*

**Ending C: "???" (Ultra-Hidden / Teaser)**
Requirements unknown. Possibly tied to:
- Solving every scannable mystery on the planet
- A community puzzle (like Animal Well's mosaic)
- Something buried in the game's data that players won't find for months/years

The ending hints at something beyond this planet and this cycle. Other worlds. Other Dragons. Other Adams. A larger story. Could tease a sequel or expansion. Could simply ask a question that has no answer.

*The game should always be a little bit unknowable.*
