# Tools & Equipment — Three-Tier Expressions

Every tool has three expressions across Tech/Bio/Cipher. The planet weans Adam off technology — tools reflect that progression. Bio versions feel like *you* instead of equipment. Cipher versions feel like reality bending to your will.

## Early Game (Minutes 0–30)

### 1. Grapple Gun ✅
- **Tech**: Mechanical pendulum swing. Hook, rope, physics. Reel in/out, pump momentum.
- **Bio**: Not needed — wall climb, cartwheel, glide cover all traversal.
- **Cipher**: Phase-step teleport. Press E → instant blink to mouse position. 350px range, 0.5s cooldown. Phases through terrain. Purple afterimage trail.
- **Lore**: Cipher tier internalizes the mechanism. The tool becomes part of Adam.

### 2. Scanner (EVE) ✅
- **Tech (Level 1)**: Passive scan. Basic creature ID, weak point highlight.
- **Bio (Level 2)**: Active scan. Behavioral data, ecological role, emotional state.
- **Cipher (Level 3)**: Deep scan. True nature revealed. Same creature scanned in different areas shows different truths.
- **Risk/reward**: Deeper scans require EVE in exposed position.

### 3. Combat Knife ✅
- **Tech**: SF6-style rapid stab. Forward thrust. Fast, precise, low damage.
- **Bio**: Overhead arc swing. Wider reach, more aggressive.
- **Cipher**: Double-slash ping-pong. Fastest, most damaging.

### 4. Sidearm
- **Tech**: Standard issue pistol. Reliable, accurate, limited ammo.
- **Bio**: Seed launcher? Thorn spitter? Biological ranged option — slower, organic projectiles, possibly with status effects (poison, slow).
- **Cipher**: Resonance pulse. Short-range energy burst. No ammo, but draws Dragon attention.
- **Design note**: Ranged combat should feel different from melee, not just "melee but far away." Tech sidearm = tactical (cover, peek, aim). Bio ranged = area denial. Cipher ranged = high risk/high reward.

## Mid Game

### 5. Lantern / Light Module
- **Tech**: Deployable light source. Attracts moths/photosensitive creatures. Repels shadow creatures. Battery-limited.
- **Bio**: Bioluminescent gland. Integrated into Adam's body. Softer light, attracts different creatures than tech version. Infinite but dim.
- **Cipher**: Adam glows — but so does everything dangerous nearby. Double-edged: you see them, they see you. Reveals hidden cipher-layer creatures (Poltergeists).
- **Gameplay**: Dark areas are genuinely dark. Light source choice affects what you encounter.

### 6. Resonance Probe
- **Tech**: Handheld device. Stab into terrain → sonar ping reveals nearby caves, ore deposits, creature dens within ~200px radius. 5s cooldown. Shows on minimap briefly.
- **Bio**: Press hand to ground. Feel vibrations. Reveals living things (creatures, root networks, water flow). Longer range but only biological signatures.
- **Cipher**: You just *know*. Passive awareness of nearby points of interest. No activation needed. But information is cryptic/symbolic — requires interpretation.
- **Gameplay**: Exploration tool. Rewards curiosity. Different tiers reveal different layers of the same world.

### 7. Trap Kit
- **Tech**: Proximity mines (damage + stun), tripwire alarms (alert EVE), deployable barricades. Limited inventory. Crafted from salvage.
- **Bio**: Pheromone lures (attract specific creature types), seed pods (grow barriers/platforms over time), spore clouds (confuse/slow).
- **Cipher**: Temporal snares (slow enemies in radius), gravity wells (pull enemies to point), phase traps (enemies phase out briefly — removed from play but also can't be damaged).
- **Gameplay**: Prep-based combat. Set up an area before engaging. Tech = predictable/reliable. Bio = chaotic/organic. Cipher = weird/powerful.

## Late Game

### 8. Breathing Apparatus
- **Tech**: Rebreather module. Timer-based underwater exploration. Suit integrity degrades faster in water.
- **Bio**: Enhanced lungs. Much longer breath. Eventually: amphibious. Can detect underwater currents and safe paths.
- **Cipher**: You don't breathe. Water is just another medium. Full underwater freedom. But water conducts Resonance — Dragon awareness spikes underwater.
- **Gameplay**: Water areas gated by this. Each tier changes HOW you interact with water, not just IF.

### 9. Gravity Anchor
- **Tech**: Device that creates local gravity field. Walk on walls/ceilings for 10s. Heavy power draw. Suit integrity cost.
- **Bio**: Grip pads evolve into full surface adhesion. Passive wall/ceiling walking. Slower than ground movement.
- **Cipher**: Gravity is a suggestion. Full omnidirectional movement. Float, walk on any surface, change "down" at will. Limited duration (concentration-based).
- **Gameplay**: Late-game traversal that opens up vertical level design. Tech = tactical positioning. Bio = permanent but limited. Cipher = brief godlike freedom.

## Design Principles

1. **Every tool has three expressions** — same problem, different fantasy.
2. **Tech degrades** — tools have integrity, battery, ammo. Maintenance required.
3. **Bio integrates** — tools become part of Adam. No inventory management, but slower/subtler.
4. **Cipher transcends** — tools are unnecessary in their original form. Reality bends instead. But always a cost (Dragon attention, Resonance buildup, concentration).
5. **No tool is strictly better** — each tier expression has tradeoffs. Tech is reliable and predictable. Bio is sustainable and organic. Cipher is powerful but dangerous.
6. **Tools affect ecology** — creatures react to your equipment. Tech lantern attracts different fauna than bio glow. Trap types change predator behavior around you.
7. **Discovery, not shops** — tools are found, grown, or understood. Never purchased. The planet provides.
